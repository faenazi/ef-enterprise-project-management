## Scope Commitment

The project documentation must cover the complete target-state system before implementation planning is treated as ready.

The scope includes the full lifecycle from strategy and demand intake through portfolio and program governance, project initiation, planning, procurement, execution, monitoring, control, acceptance, closure, and benefits realization. It must also cover all supporting data, integrations, roles, workflows, controls, security, reporting, user experience, non-functional requirements, operations, testing, release, and transition needs.

Incremental releases and Agile sprints may be used to implement the documented scope. They must not be used to omit capabilities from the target-state requirements or to redefine the project as an MVP without an explicit documented decision.

## Documentation Readiness Checklist

Implementation may begin only when the scope selected for a sprint satisfies all applicable checks:

- [ ] Required documents in the status matrix are `Ready` or `N/A` with a reason.
- [ ] The complete target-state scope remains documented even when delivery is incremental.
- [ ] Current-state and target-state processes are sufficiently understood for the selected scope.
- [ ] Scope, business requirements, business rules, functional requirements, NFRs, exceptions, and acceptance criteria are sufficiently documented.
- [ ] Users, roles, data scopes, approvals, delegations, and segregation-of-duties rules are documented.
- [ ] Required architecture, data, integration, security, operational, and UI/UX decisions are recorded.
- [ ] Systems of record, ownership, contracts, synchronization, reconciliation, and failure behavior are defined.
- [ ] Backlog items selected for the sprint meet the Definition of Ready.
- [ ] Traceability has no unresolved gap that blocks implementation of the selected scope.
- [ ] QA strategy, scenarios, planned cases, evidence expectations, required environments, and test data are defined.
- [ ] Material dependencies, risks, assumptions, constraints, and open questions have owners and actions.
- [ ] No unresolved blocker requires the delivery team or an AI tool to invent a business or technical decision.

This checklist records readiness for implementation and does not represent a formal approval or signature.

- Do not invent missing information.
